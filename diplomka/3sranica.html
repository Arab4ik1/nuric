Top 5 Rare Models
Top 1

Mercedes 500 K Cabriolet A (1935)

And this luxurious and fast 1935 500 K Cabriolet A is a coveted artifact for wealthy collectors of pre-war Mercedes. The letter K in its name stands for Kompressor. In total, 36 of these convertibles were produced, equipped with 160-horsepower 5-liter in-line "eights", which fully justify the auction price of the car, which went under the hammer for 2.7 million euros in 2015.
Top 2

Mercedes 540 K Special Roadster (1937)

Having reached almost the very top of the shortlist, we again see a car from the 1930s. Sold in 2016 for 9 million euros, this 1937 540 K Special Roadster is rightfully considered the most expensive pre-war Mercedes. The fantastic Kompressor Spezial Roadster is powered by a 180-horsepower 5.4-liter supercharged 8-cylinder engine. In his 82 years, he has traveled a total of 16,000 km.
Top 3

Mercedes 680 S Torpedo Roadster (1929)

And once again we return to the distant and glorious past of cars with a three-pointed star on the hood, but this time with a Mercedes 680 S Torpedo Roadster from 1929 with a body from the famous French studio Saoutchik.

Top 4

Mercedes CLK GTR (1998)

And here is another relatively young Mercedes, which got into our TOP-10. This is a very rare 1988 CLK GTR that changed hands last year for 4.1 million euros. The mid-engined sports two-door was designed and built by AMG specialists in the amount of only 25 units for homologation in the new FIA GT championship series. Initially costing 1.5 million euros and having traveled a little over 1,400 km during this time, the car, which eventually left for a record amount, is equipped with a 6.9-liter V12 engine with a capacity of 612 forces.
Top 5

Mercedes W196R F1 (1954)

And topping the list of the most expensive Mercedes-Benz of all time is not an ordinary road car, but a racing legend that, in 1954, allowed Juan Manuel Fangio to become F1 world champion for the second time. We're talking about a single-seat car that won both the German and Swiss GPs in 1954. It was sold at a Bonhams auction in 2013 for €22 million. That is, at the price of the best classic Ferrari models.
